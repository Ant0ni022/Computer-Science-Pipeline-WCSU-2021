Storage Device

Alternatively referred to as digital storage, storage, storage
media, or storage medium, a storage device is any hardware
capable of holding information either temporarily or permanently.
The picture shows an example of a Drobo, an external secondary
mass storage device.

There are two types of storage devices used with computers: a primary
storage device, such as RAM, and a secondary storage device, such as
a hard drive. Secondary storage can be removable, internal, or
external.

Why is storage needed in a computer?

Without a storage device, a computer cannot save or remember any
settings or information and would be considered a dumb terminal.

Although a computer can run with no storage device, it would only be able
to view information, unless it was connected to another computer that had
storage capabilities. Even a task, such as browsing the Internet, requires
information to be stored on your computer.
