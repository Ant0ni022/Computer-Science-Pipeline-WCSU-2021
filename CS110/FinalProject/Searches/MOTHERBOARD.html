Motherboard

Alternatively referred to as the mb, mainboard, mboard,
mobo, mobd, backplane board, base board, main circuit
board, planar board, system board, or a logic board on
Apple computers. The motherboard is a printed circuit board
and foundation of a computer that is the biggest board in a
computer chassis. It allocates power and allows communication
to and between the CPU, RAM, and all other computer hardware
components.

What was the first motherboard?

The first motherboard is considered to be one used in the IBM Personal
Computer, released in 1981. At the time, IBM called it a "planar" instead
of a motherboard. The IBM Personal Computer and the motherboard inside
it would set the standard for IBM-compatible computer hardware going
forward.
